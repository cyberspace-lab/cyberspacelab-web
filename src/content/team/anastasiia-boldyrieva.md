I am an undergraduate student at the Faculty of Humanities, Charles University. In the CyberspaceLab, I’ve been able to combine my interest in unmanned aerial vehicles with psychology. My thesis, “Cognitive Testing of FPV Drone Pilots,” focuses on the selection process for professional FPV pilots. Each day of working on this project gives me an opportunity to acquire new skills – from designing questionnaires to learning to fly drones myself.


Outside of the Lab, I am an active Scout, which means I spend most of my free time organizing trips or cultural events, while handling a bunch of paperwork, and keeping up with the texts from 50 volunteers I coordinate.
